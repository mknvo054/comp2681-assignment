Calgary

Calgary is a vibrant and modern city known for its blend of urban sophistication and Western heritage. As the largest city in Alberta, Calgary is famous for hosting the Calgary Stampede, a world-renowned rodeo and festival celebrating cowboy culture with thrilling events, live music, and delicious food. The city's downtown skyline is marked by the iconic Calgary Tower, offering panoramic views of the city and nearby Rocky Mountains. Nature lovers can explore Prince's Island Park, a scenic urban park along the Bow River, or take a short drive to the stunning Banff National Park. Calgary is also a hub for sports, being home to the NHL's Calgary Flames and the CFL's Calgary Stampeders, making it an exciting destination for sports fans. With a strong economy fueled by the energy sector, a growing tech industry, and a high quality of life, Calgary continues to be a dynamic and prosperous city.

Edmonton

Edmonton, the capital of Alberta, is known for its rich cultural scene, stunning river valley, and thriving festival culture. The city hosts numerous events throughout the year, earning it the nickname "Festival City", with highlights such as the Edmonton International Fringe Festival, one of the largest in the world. The West Edmonton Mall, one of the biggest shopping and entertainment centers in North America, features an indoor amusement park, water park, and countless shops and restaurants. Edmonton's River Valley Parks system, the largest stretch of urban parkland in Canada, offers endless opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and kayaking. The city is also home to the NHL's Edmonton Oilers, a team with a passionate hockey fan base. As a center for government, education, and innovation, Edmonton plays a crucial role in Alberta's economy while maintaining a welcoming and diverse community.
